# Zadonetske — at a glance
**Coordinates:** 49.6407° N, 36.3526° E  
**Administration:** Zmiiv urban hromada, Chuhuiv district, Kharkiv region  
**Local form of the name:** Zadinecke (from the local name of the Siverskyi Donets River — Dinec)

The village stretches along the left bank of the Siverskyi Donets and is surrounded by pine forests of the Homilshanski Lisy National Park. Floodplain lakes (including Bile Lake), sandy beaches and scenic viewpoints make it perfect for quiet nature breaks, walks and fishing.

> Note: open sources list the pre‑2020 population at around 690; current figures to be verified.
## Legends & local stories

- **Kozacha Hora (Koropove hamlets).** Local lore speaks of Cossack battles here; some versions retell the dragon myth with the hero Nykyta Kozhemiaka. The hill offers one of the best views over the Siverskyi Donets valley.  
- **Ancient settlements along the Donets.** Numerous archaeological sites are recorded on the left bank between Zadonetske and Cherkaskyi Byshkyn — from the Neolithic and Eneolithic to Kyivan Rus and the Cossack era.  
- **Village origins.** Written sources date the founding to the late 17th century (around 1680). By the 19th century there was a church and a brickworks; by the early 20th century the population exceeded a thousand.
